What does it do?

Whenever a natural or man-made disaster strikes a country, aid workers rush from around the globe to stop the suffering. Unfortunately, most of them do not speak the language of those they are trying to serve.

Meanwhile, members of the affected community who are geographically removed from the crisis' epicenter are often unable to contribute beyond watching on television and wiring money home.

Speakeasy provides a local phone number for relief workers to call during emergency situations to receive a real human translator to guide them through the language barrier.

How does it work?

Speakeasy uses the Twilio internet calling API to connect callers cheaply anywhere in the world. A high-concurrency node.js server handles and routes calls to ensure fast service when it's needed.
